Explore Tunisia

Tunisia is a North African country located between the Mediterranean Sea and the Sahara Desert, offering a fascinating mix of ancient history, diverse landscapes, and a unique blend of Mediterranean and Arab cultures.
From stunning beaches to desert dunes, from Roman ruins to vibrant souks, Tunisia captivates every traveler with its rich heritage and warm hospitality.

From Carthage to Today

814 BC

Carthage

Founded by Queen Elissa (Dido), Carthage became a powerful Mediterranean civilization and rival of Rome.

146 BC

Roman Tunisia

After defeating Carthage, Rome made Tunisia one of its richest provinces, building amphitheaters, aqueducts, and cities.

647 AD

Arab Conquest

The arrival of Islam transformed Tunisia, establishing Arab-Islamic culture and founding Kairouan, one of the holiest cities in Islam.

1574

Ottoman Era

Tunisia became part of the Ottoman Empire, developing a unique blend of Turkish, Arab, and Mediterranean cultures.

1881

French Protectorate

Tunisia became a French protectorate, leading to 75 years of colonial rule that shaped modern Tunisian society and infrastructure.

1956

Independence

Led by Habib Bourguiba, Tunisia gained independence on March 20, 1956, becoming a modern republic with progressive reforms.

Did you know?

Fascinating facts about Tunisia that will surprise you!

1

Tunisia is the smallest country in North Africa, but it packs incredible diversity within its borders.

2

The Sahara Desert covers almost 40% of Tunisia's territory, offering breathtaking desert landscapes.

3

Djerba is home to one of the oldest Jewish communities in the world, with the famous El Ghriba Synagogue.

4

The Roman Amphitheatre of El Jem is one of the largest in the world and can hold up to 35,000 spectators.

5

Star Wars was filmed in Tunisia! The desert landscapes of Tataouine inspired the planet Tatooine.

6

Tunisia has 8 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, showcasing its rich historical and cultural heritage.

7

Harissa, the famous spicy chili paste, originated in Tunisia and is now loved worldwide.

8

Tunisia has one of the highest literacy rates in Africa, with over 80% of the population able to read and write.
